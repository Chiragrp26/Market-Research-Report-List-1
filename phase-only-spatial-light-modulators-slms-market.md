<p><p>Phase-only spatial light modulators (SLMs) are devices used to control the phase of light waves. There are two main types of SLMs in the market based on their mirror structure: dielectric mirror type and no-dielectric mirror type. </p><p>Dielectric mirror SLMs use a mirror with a dielectric coating, which enhances the reflectivity and reduces light loss. This results in improved performance in terms of phase control and efficiency.</p><p>On the other hand, no-dielectric mirror SLMs do not have a dielectric coating. While they have lower reflectivity and higher light loss, they are typically more cost-effective compared to dielectric mirror SLMs.</p><p>Both types of SLMs offer different trade-offs in terms of performance and cost, allowing users to choose a suitable option based on their specific needs.</p></p>
