<p><p>The automotive rear-view reversing cameras market encompasses two main types: CCD cameras and CMOS cameras. CCD cameras, or Charge Coupled Device cameras, are known for their high-quality image resolution, allowing for clear images even in low-light conditions. On the other hand, CMOS cameras, or Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor cameras, are more cost-effective and consume less power compared to CCD cameras. While CMOS cameras may not provide the same level of image quality as CCD cameras, they still offer satisfactory resolution and performance for rear-view reversing applications.</p></p>
