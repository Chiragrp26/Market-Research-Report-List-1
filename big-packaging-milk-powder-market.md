<p><p>The big packaging milk powder market consists of two main types: skim milk powder and whole milk powder. Skim milk powder is produced by drying skim milk, removing the fat content, and eventually turning into a powder form. It is commonly used as a low-fat alternative in various food and beverage products. On the other hand, whole milk powder is made by evaporating the water content of milk, resulting in a shelf-stable powder containing the full-fat content of milk. It is often used in applications where a higher fat content is desired, such as in baking or dairy products.</p></p>
