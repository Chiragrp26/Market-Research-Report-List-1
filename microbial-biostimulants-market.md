<p><p>Microbial biostimulants are substances that enhance plant growth and development by stimulating the activity of beneficial microorganisms in the soil. Natural biostimulants are derived from organic sources and include substances like seaweed extracts, humic acids, and beneficial microorganisms. Synthetic biostimulants, on the other hand, are chemically synthesized compounds that mimic the effects of natural biostimulants. Both types of biostimulants aim to improve plant health, nutrient uptake, and overall crop productivity, but they differ in their composition and mode of action.</p></p>
