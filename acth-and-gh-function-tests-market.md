<p><p>ACTH (adrenocorticotropic hormone) and GH (growth hormone) function tests are diagnostic tests used to evaluate the function of the adrenal glands and pituitary gland, respectively. </p><p>ACTH function tests are used to assess the production and regulation of cortisol, a hormone produced by the adrenal glands that helps regulate metabolism and stress response. These tests are commonly used to diagnose conditions such as Addison's disease and Cushing's syndrome.</p><p>GH function tests, on the other hand, are used to evaluate the production and regulation of growth hormone, which is responsible for promoting growth and development. These tests are often performed to diagnose growth hormone deficiency or excess, as well as monitoring the effectiveness of GH replacement therapy.</p><p>The ACTH and GH function tests market is expected to witness substantial growth in the coming years.
