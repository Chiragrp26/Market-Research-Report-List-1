<p><p>The liquid pouch packaging market is categorized into two types: rigid and flexible. Rigid packaging refers to containers made of firm, inflexible materials such as plastic or glass. It offers excellent protection and structural integrity, making it suitable for storing liquids that require stability. On the other hand, flexible packaging is made from pliable materials like plastic films or laminates. It offers convenience, versatility, and often features spouts or resealable openings. Flexible packaging is commonly used for single-use or smaller quantities of liquid products.</p></p>
