<p><p>A wheel speed sensor is an electronic device that helps in measuring the rotational speed of a vehicle's wheels. It is an essential component of the Anti-lock Braking System (ABS) and Traction Control System (TCS), as it provides crucial data to these systems to prevent wheel lock-up during braking and loss of traction. The wheel speed sensor is typically installed near each wheel and consists of a magnetic or Hall-effect sensor along with a toothed ring or tone wheel attached to the wheel hub or axle.</p><p>The wheel speed sensor market is witnessing substantial growth due to the increasing adoption of advanced safety systems in vehicles. <p><p>The Wheel Speed Sensor market comprises two prominent types: Hall Wheel Speed Sensor and Magnetic Electric Wheel Speed Sensor. The Hall Wheel Speed Sensor employs Hall effect technology to detect changes in the magnetic field caused by wheel rotation, providing accurate speed readings. Similarly, the Magnetic Electric Wheel Speed Sensor uses magnets and electric currents to determine the rotational speed of the wheel. Both sensor types play a vital role in various industries such as automotive, aerospace, and robotics by enabling precise measurement and monitoring of wheel speed.</p></p>
