<p><p>Image Guided Radiation Therapy (IGRT) is a technology that uses real-time imaging to precisely deliver radiation therapy to a tumor. It enables the accurate targeting of tumors while minimizing damage to surrounding healthy tissues. IGRT involves the use of imaging techniques such as X-rays, CT scans, and MRIs to create detailed images of the tumor's location, shape, and size. This allows radiation oncologists to make the necessary adjustments to deliver the radiation beams precisely.</p><p>The future outlook for the IGRT market is optimistic. The increasing prevalence of cancer worldwide, along with the growing demand for precise and effective radiation therapy, is driving the market's growth. Additionally, advancements in imaging technology and software, as well as the integration of IGRT with other treatment modalities, are expected to further propel market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the rising adoption of IGRT by healthcare providers and the continuous development of new and improved IGRT systems are contributing to market expansion.
