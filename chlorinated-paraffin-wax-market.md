<p><p>Chlorinated Paraffin Wax is available in three different market types: L-grade, M-grade, and H-grade. L-grade refers to the low chlorination level and is mainly used in the production of plasticizers and additives for vinyl, rubber, and flame retardants. M-grade signifies a medium chlorination level and is commonly employed as an additive in lubricating oils and metalworking fluids. Lastly, H-grade denotes the highest chlorination level and is primarily utilized as a flame retardant in plastics, rubber, and paints. Each market type caters to different applications based on their chlorination levels.</p></p>
