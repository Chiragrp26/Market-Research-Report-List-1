<p><p>Refinish paints and coatings are categorized into three main types: water-based coatings, solvent-based coatings, and "other" market coatings.</p><p>Water-based coatings are made by dissolving synthetic or natural resins in water, making them environmentally friendly and low in VOC emissions. They are commonly used in automotive and industrial applications and offer excellent durability and adhesion.</p><p>Solvent-based coatings are produced by dissolving resins in solvents like alcohol or hydrocarbons. They provide superior chemical and abrasion resistance and are widely used in applications requiring fast drying times and long-lasting finishes.</p><p>The "other" market includes alternative coating technologies such as powder coatings, radiation-cured coatings, and other specialty coatings. These coatings are known for their unique properties, such as high temperature resistance, anti-graffiti capabilities, or specialized adhesion properties, and are used in specific industries where their characteristics are advantageous.</p></p>
