<p><p>Aramid fibers, commonly known as "super fibers," can be categorized into two main types: para-aramid and meta-aramid. Para-aramid fibers, such as Kevlar, possess high strength and heat resistance, making them ideal for applications requiring durability, such as bulletproof vests and tires. On the other hand, meta-aramid fibers, like Nomex, exhibit excellent flame resistance and thermal stability, making them suitable for industries where protection against high heat and flames is crucial, such as firefighting gear and industrial clothing. Both types of aramid fibers have specific properties that cater to different market needs.</p></p>
