<p><p>Polyurethanes are used in the footwear market due to their versatility and benefits. Methylene Diphenyl Diisocyanate (MDI) and Toluene Diphenyl Diisocyanate (TDI) are isocyanates used in the production of polyurethanes. MDI is favored for its high performance and durability, while TDI is known for its flexibility. Polyols, on the other hand, are a key ingredient in polyurethane production and act as a reactant with the isocyanates. Polyols determine the final properties of the polyurethane, such as hardness and elasticity. Together, these components contribute to the wide range of polyurethane-based footwear available in the market.</p></p>
