<p><p>The biorefinery market comprises two major types: biochemical and thermochemical. Biochemical biorefineries utilize biological processes such as fermentation and enzymatic reactions to convert biomass into various products, including fuels, chemicals, and materials. Thermochemical biorefineries employ heat, catalysts, and pressure to convert biomass into liquid, gaseous, or solid fuels and chemicals. Both types aim to maximize the value and utilization of biomass resources, contributing to a more sustainable and environmentally friendly economy by reducing reliance on fossil fuels and minimizing waste generation.</p></p>
