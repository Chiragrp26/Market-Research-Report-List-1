<p><ul><li>Contact</li><li>Contactless</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Non-wearable sleep monitoring devices can be categorized into two types: contact and contactless. Contact sleep monitoring devices typically involve sensors or electrodes that come into direct contact with the user's body, such as wristbands or headbands. These devices measure physiological signals like heart rate and movement to track sleep patterns. On the other hand, contactless sleep monitoring devices utilize technologies like radar or cameras to capture data without physical contact. These devices monitor sleep by analyzing factors like breathing patterns, body movement, and even environmental parameters in the sleeping area.</p></p>
