<p><p>Radiofrequency Identification (RFID) is a technology utilized for automated data collection, tracking, and identification purposes. The RFID market is categorized into three main types: Low Frequency (LF), Moderate Frequency (MF), and High Frequency (HF). LF operates in between 125-134 kHz, MF operates between 13.56 MHz, and HF operates at 860-960 MHz. LF RFID is commonly used for access control systems and animal tracking, while MF RFID is utilized in contactless payment systems and identification cards. HF RFID finds applicability in ticketing, asset tracking, and supply chain management. Each frequency variant serves different applications with varying data rates and read ranges.</p></p>
