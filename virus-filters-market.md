<p><p>Virus filters are divided into three main market types based on the phase in which they operate - Aqueous Phase, Gaseous Phase, and Organic Phase. Aqueous Phase filters are designed to remove viruses from liquids, such as water, pharmaceuticals, and beverages. Gaseous Phase filters are used to filter viruses present in the air and gases, commonly found in cleanrooms and air handling systems. Organic Phase filters are utilized to eliminate viruses from organic solvents and chemicals used in various industries like pharmaceuticals, food and beverage, and biotechnology. These market types cater to specific filtration needs in different industries.</p></p>
