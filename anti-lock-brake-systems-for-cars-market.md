<p><p>An Anti-lock Brake System (ABS) is a safety feature in cars that prevents the wheels from locking up during braking. The market for ABS systems can be classified into four types based on the number of channels involved. One-channel ABS uses a single channel to control all the brakes on a car. Two-channel ABS has two separate channels controlling the front and rear brakes independently. Three-channel ABS has three channels controlling each wheel separately, while four-channel ABS involves four channels controlling each wheel independently, offering the highest level of control and safety.</p></p>
