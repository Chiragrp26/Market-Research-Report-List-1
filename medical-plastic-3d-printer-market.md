<p><p>Medical plastic 3D printer market types can be categorized into Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM), Fused Granular Fabrication (FGF), Laser Melting/Fusing (LMF), and others. FDM printers utilize a heated nozzle to deposit melted plastic layer by layer, allowing for precise fabrication of complex medical structures. FGF printers build objects by extruding plastic granules and shaping them through a heated nozzle. LMF printers employ lasers to selectively melt metal powder, creating intricate medical components. Other types include stereolithography (SLA) that use liquid resin solidification and selective laser sintering (SLS) which fuses powdered plastic materials.</p></p>
