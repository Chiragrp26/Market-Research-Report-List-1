<p><p>Monk fruit extract is available in the market in two forms: solid and liquid. Solid monk fruit extract is produced by drying the fruit and grinding it into a powder. It is commonly used as a natural sweetener in baking and cooking. On the other hand, liquid monk fruit extract is produced by steeping the fruit in a liquid, typically alcohol or water, and then straining it. It is commonly used as a sweetener in beverages and can be added to various food products. Both solid and liquid forms offer a natural and calorie-free alternative to sugar.</p></p>
