Single-phase CVTs are typically used in residential and small commercial applications, providing a continuous and stable output voltage to protect sensitive equipment from voltage fluctuations. On the other hand, three-phase CVTs are used in industrial and larger commercial settings, ensuring a stable output voltage for heavy machinery, motors, and other power-intensive applications. Both types play a vital role in maintaining a consistent power supply and protecting electrical devices from damage.</p></p>
