<p><p>The biodegradable plastic market consists of different types of materials. Polylactic Acid (PLA) is derived from renewable resources like corn starch, and is commonly used in packaging and disposable items. Polybutylene Succinate (PBS) is another biodegradable plastic made from fossil fuels and is used in the manufacture of films and packaging materials. Polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA) are bioplastics produced by bacteria and can be used in various applications. Cellulose derivatives, derived from cellulose found in plant cell walls, are also used in biodegradable plastics. Other types of biodegradable plastics exist, but are not as widely used.</p></p>
