<p><p>Intermetallic Alloy Market Types can be categorized into four main groups: aluminium-based, copper-based, nickel-based, and others. Aluminium-based intermetallic alloys are a combination of aluminium with different elements such as titanium or chromium, and are known for their lightweight and high-temperature resistance. Copper-based intermetallic alloys contain copper as the main component and are used in applications requiring high electrical conductivity and corrosion resistance. Nickel-based intermetallic alloys, on the other hand, are composed of nickel combined with elements like titanium or chromium, and are known for their exceptional strength and high-temperature stability. Finally, the "others" category includes intermetallic alloys that do not fit into the three mentioned groups, such as iron-based alloys or cobalt-based alloys.</p></p>
