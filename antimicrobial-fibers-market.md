<p><p>Antimicrobial fibers are categorized into two types: natural antibacterial fiber and chemical antibacterial fiber. Natural antibacterial fibers are derived from naturally occurring materials, such as bamboo and silver nanoparticles, which possess inherent antimicrobial properties. These fibers do not require the addition of any chemicals or treatments. On the other hand, chemical antibacterial fibers are produced by incorporating antimicrobial agents into synthetic fibers during the manufacturing process. These fibers are designed to inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi, making them suitable for various applications in the textile and apparel industry.</p></p>
