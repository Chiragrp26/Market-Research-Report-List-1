<p><p>Optical transponders are devices used in optical fiber communication systems to convert optical signals to electrical signals and vice versa. They are available in various market types, including 155 Mbps, 2.5 Gbps, 10 Gbps, and others. The market types represent the data transfer rates supported by the transponders. For instance, a 155 Mbps transponder can handle data transfers at a rate of 155 million bits per second. Similarly, 2.5 Gbps and 10 Gbps transponders can handle data transfers at rates of 2.5 billion and 10 billion bits per second respectively. The "other" market type encompasses transponders with alternative data transfer rates.</p></p>
