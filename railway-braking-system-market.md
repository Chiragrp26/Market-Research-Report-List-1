<p><p>The railway braking system market consists of four main types: pneumatic brake, electrodynamics brake, mechanical brake, and electromagnetic brake. Pneumatic brakes function by using compressed air to activate the braking system. Electrodynamics brakes use electrical current to generate the braking force. Mechanical brakes involve using the physical force created by friction or pressure to slow down the train. Electromagnetic brakes use a magnetic field to produce a braking force. These types differ in the way they generate and apply force to stop the train.</p></p>
