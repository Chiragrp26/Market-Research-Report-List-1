<p><p>A smoke damper is a component of the HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning) system that prevents the spread of smoke within a building during a fire. It is designed to detect smoke and automatically close to restrict the movement of smoke and fire. Smoke dampers are typically installed in ducts and are controlled by a smoke detection system or fire alarm.</p><p>The smoke damper market has witnessed significant growth in recent years due to the increasing awareness of fire safety and the strict regulations imposed by governments and regulatory bodies. The market is driven by the rising demand for smoke control systems in commercial buildings, hospitals, educational institutions, and other public places.</p><p>Furthermore, the construction industry's growth and the increasing investment in infrastructure development have also contributed to the market's expansion. As more buildings are being constructed, the demand for smoke dampers is expected to rise to comply with fire safety codes and regulations.</p><p>In terms of market analysis, the smoke damper market is expected to grow at a Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 7% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to the continuous advancements in smoke damper technology, such as the integration of smart sensors and control systems, which enhance the efficiency and reliability of smoke dampers.</p><p>Moreover, there is a growing trend towards the adoption of smoke dampers in retrofit building projects to improve the fire safety measures in older structures. This trend, alongside the increasing focus on sustainable building practices, is expected to drive the market growth further.</p><p>Overall, the smoke damper market is poised for steady growth in the coming years, supported by factors such as stringent fire safety regulations, infrastructure development, technological advancements, and increasing awareness of smoke control systems.</p></p>

<p><p>The Smoke Damper Market can be categorized into two types: Manual Type and Motorized Type. Manual type smoke dampers are operated manually, usually through a lever or handle, to open or close the damper. These dampers are suitable for smaller applications and are less expensive. On the other hand, motorized type smoke dampers have a motor attached, allowing them to be operated remotely using control systems. These dampers are more suitable for larger buildings and provide more convenience and control.</p></p>
